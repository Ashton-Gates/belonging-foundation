Our Mission


The Belonging Foundation is a non-profit organization that empowers innovators to create, transform, and disrupt industries by unleashing the power of a more connected community

LOVE YOUR DREAMERS

Innovators have always shaper the history of technology. And no matter the future, innovators will build it.



Guided by code values

We use our core values every day to guide how we collaborate with each other and operate as a whole, and look for them in prospective candidates.


Think Big, Go Far

We are big dreamers with a passion for creativity. We eagerly pursue new opportunities and markets through innovation and disruption. We have a pioneering spirit - always ready to forge new paths and take smart risks.

WHAT IT ISN'T

  • Letting the magnitude of any challenge squelch fresh thinking and ideas
  • Change merely for the sake of change
  • Grand ideas without a coherent plan
  • Believing valuable ideas can only come from senior people

WHAT IT IS

  • Fearless in our belief that we have a massive opportunity
  • Willingness to experiment with crazy ideas, and recognize not all experiments work
  • Eager to question the status quo
  • Disrupting ourselves before someone else does


What we do

The Belonging Foundation is a digital safe space for Odd-Balls, Plant-Lovers, Black-Sheep, Lions, and the like to turn their dreams for tomorrow into actionable plans for the future and secure community support and engagement while doing so.

Employees
We have amazing people contributing to our success.

The Belonging Foundation exists today because if it had existed 4 years sooner, it may have changed my family's story. Kevin, Keegan, Alvin, Judy, and Eric DeLong are a cluster of 5 immediate family members who passed naturally, tragically, and for some, avoidably all within a mere 15 months of one another. These were a lovingly (and grumpily) married set of parents, Alvin (Age 87) and Judith DeLong (Age 80), their "Philosopher" son, Eric DeLong (Age 57), their "Never Let the Bastards Wear You Down" son, Kevin DeLong (Age 54), and his "Lions, Not Sheep" son, Keegan DeLong (Forever Age 22).

The DeLongs grew up in Central Illinois and raged about Illinois politics and the Chicago Bears for generations. Alvin was an avid marathon runner and at one point, a Golden Glove Boxer, in addition to spending his working years at the GE Plastics Factory in Streator, IL. Eric learned as much as he could about the world through books and collegiate pursuits at Illinois State University.

Eric even served as President of the Streator Lions for a time. Judith's heart carried a flame that she protected increasingly over the years, and was the grandma who liked to catch up on the phone for hours and hours on a late afternoon. Kevin DeLong lived tall in every minute of his 54 years.

Kevin rode motorcycles with his sons and Iron Pig Brothers all around the country for the majority of his adult life, and put in over 20 years of tireless effort at the Pontiac Correctional Center in Pontiac, IL. From the day he was born, Keegan DeLong just wanted to love and be loved. At 22 years old, he was a young man charting a fiery path ahead to match his blazing hair and past, weighing the dreams of his forefathers against those still forming in his entrepreneurial, giving heart.

This Foundation was formed to help men and women at life stages such as these who are looking for help in realizing their most hope-filled dreams of a brighter, better tomorrow. Together we will create the future, so let's honor their memories by building a network community working together to build a future we're excited to live for.
